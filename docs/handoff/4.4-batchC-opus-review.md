## 1. The bug (fixed): 反间 gave the transfer backwards

`server/src/content/skills/fanjian.ts`.

**What it did:** the target named a suit, then **周瑜 took one of the _target's_ hand cards into his
own hand** (chose from `cardChoicesFor(G, target)`, moved `→ source`), and dealt 1 damage on a suit
mismatch.

**What 反间 actually is** — and this is not a judgement call, the team's own authoritative 4.1a
cross-check settles it: the locale text (which §11 makes the tie-breaker when §8's gloss is ambiguous)
says the opposite direction in **both** languages:
- `zh.json`: `该角色获得你的一张手牌` — "that character gains one of **your** hand cards."
- `en.json`: "another character names a suit, then takes one of **your** hand cards and reveals it."

周瑜 **gives** the target one of **his own** cards (revealed); a suit mismatch is what lets him deal
1 damage. He does not steal from the target. §8's terse "takes a hand card of your choosing" reads
ambiguously in either direction; the locale disambiguates, and the implementation went the wrong way.
This is a real power-level change, not a cosmetic one (the impl was effectively 顺手牵羊-plus-damage; the
real skill is a forced-gift-plus-damage), and it also mis-used the hidden-hand slot protocol to look
into the target's hand when 周瑜 is supposed to be choosing from his own (visible) hand.

**Fix applied** (three surgical edits, header comment + `targeting`/`canPlay` + the choose/transfer
block, re-reading the file immediately before writing per CONTINUE.md):
- `canPlay` now requires the **source** to hold ≥1 card (the card he gives away is the cost); the
  target's hand became irrelevant, so its old `predicate` (target must have cards) was removed — you
  can 反间 an empty-handed character.
- the `chooseCard` request now offers **`cardChoicesFor(G, source)`** with **`target: source`** (周瑜
  picks from his own hand by index — he may look and deliberately pick off-suit to guarantee the
  damage, which is the whole skill).
- the `moveCards` destination flipped to **`{z:'hand', player: target}`**, and the `log.card_taken`
  params now name the target as recipient and 周瑜 as source.

The `declareSuit → chooseCard → moveCards(+damage)` sequencing, the `once_per_turn` limit, and the
suit-mismatch check were already correct and are untouched. No other file needed changing (the bgio
`chooseCard`/`declareSuit` stages are generic and already write `chosen`/`chosenZone`/`declaredSuit`
back to the resume ctx; the fix only changes which zone those address).

**Caveat:** I could not build-verify this. The change is type-preserving (same frame shapes, different
values) and rides the existing `chooseCard`/`resolveSlot` path exactly as 3.3's 大乔/陆逊 cards do, so I
am confident, but `run-tests.bat` must confirm.

## 2. The `strike.ts` restructure + `G.turnFlags` handoff — SOUND

This was the item flagged for the most careful review. Verdict: **the turnFlag handoff is correct and
does not have a race/staleness hazard.** I verified the reasoning against `engine/pump.ts` and
`engine/triggers.ts` rather than taking it on faith. The argument, in full:

1. **The whole fan-out drains before step 2 runs.** Step 1 returns `[card.target trigger,
   resume(targeted)]` in one `pushFrames`. The trigger expands (via `fanOut`) into `triggerStep`
   frames, each of which pushes its `confirmSkill`/judge/request/effect frames — and every one of
   those sits **above** the `resume(targeted)` frame on the stack. So 铁骑's judge (→ `tieji_result`
   → the `{t:'flag'}` that sets `tieji.forceHit`) and 流离's `liuliRedirect` request (→ the move that
   writes `liuli.redirectTo`) both fully resolve **before** the `resume(targeted)` frame is ever
   popped. By the time step 2 reads the flag, it is provably set-or-not-set for this strike.

2. **A blocked request does not let anyone else interleave.** When 铁骑's `confirmSkill` or 流离's
   `liuliRedirect` sets `G.pending`, the pump halts — but the `resume(targeted)` frame stays at the
   bottom of the stack, untouched, and only the asked player can move. Their move is part of
   铁骑/流离's own flow and re-pumps into the same stack. No third player's move, and no unrelated
   engine frame, runs between the flag being set and step 2 reading it. This is the "fully synchronous
   engine, no other player's move can run mid-pump" property the impl lane relied on, and it holds.

3. **No cross-strike staleness.** Step 2 reads the flag **and clears it in the same return** (a
   `{t:'flag', value:false/undefined}` frame that pops before the demand/hit frames it precedes — the
   `strike.test.ts` assertions pin this exactly). And strikes never nest: a strike fully resolves
   before another is played (a dying window only asks for 桃; a 决斗/借刀-forced 杀 resolves
   sequentially, not inside another strike's `card.target` window). So a flag from strike A cannot
   leak into strike B.

4. **Why a public `G.strike` field (the §2.1/§12.2 pattern) is genuinely not needed here.** Those
   fields exist because a listener's answer must reach a resume frame that is **not on top** when the
   listener runs. `G.turnFlags` is playing exactly that channel role here; the only thing a
   `G.strike`-with-assert-on-reentry would buy over it is single-slot safety enforced by an assert
   instead of by sequencing — and sequencing already guarantees it because strikes provably don't
   nest. The turnFlag is the lighter correct choice. It is *marginally* more fragile than a
   field+assert (a future expansion skill that could initiate a nested strike inside a `card.target`
   window would silently cross flags rather than tripping an assert), but that is the same
   "no-expansion-assumption" caveat `G.damage`'s own single-slot assert carries, and nothing in
   Standard reaches it. **Accept as-is.** If an expansion ever does nest strikes, the migration is the
   mechanical one §2.1 already describes for `G.damage` (turn it into a small stack / a `G.strike`
   field with an assert).

**Two edge behaviours worth recording (not bugs):**

- **铁骑 + 流离 on the same strike** (马超 strikes 大乔): both are `card.target` listeners, both
  eligible; 马超 is the turn player so 铁骑 sorts first, sets `forceHit`; 大乔's 流离 then redirects.
  Step 2 computes `target = redirectTo` **and** honours `forceHit`, so the strike lands unblockably on
  the **redirected** target. Whether 铁骑's forced hit should follow a 流离 redirect is a genuinely
  ambiguous rules corner; the current behaviour is defensible but undocumented. Recommend a one-line
  note in `liuli.ts`/`strike.ts` next to the existing "no chained retarget" caveat. Minor.
- `liuli.redirectTo` is written **directly** by the `redirectStrike` move (`G.turnFlags[...] =
  newTarget`) but cleared via a `{t:'flag'}` frame in step 2 (`tieji.forceHit` is written *and* cleared
  via `{t:'flag'}`). The asymmetry is harmless (a move may mutate G) but slightly inconsistent.

## 4. 急救 as a query (deviation from §8) — UPHELD

The lane implemented 急救 as `cardsAs('peach')` gated on "not your own turn," not §8's `demand.open`
proxy sketch. This is correct:
- The locale (both languages) says exactly "Outside your own turn, you may use any red card as a
  Peach" (`zh: 你的回合外，你可以将一张红色牌当【桃】使用`), which is a `cardsAs` widening, not a proxy.
  §11 confirms this wording against sources and is the authoritative correction to §8's earlier draft.
- The own-turn gate `G.seats[G.activeSeat] === owner` is correct: `G.seats[i]` is the player at seat
  `i` and `G.activeSeat` is the current turn seat (confirmed against `triggers.ts`'s
  `seatOrderFromTurnPlayer` and CONTINUE.md's "seat = index = turn order"). So it reads false during
  Hua Tuo's own turn and true otherwise — including any other player's dying window, which is where it
  answers the existing `demand(peach)` fold. No new mechanism, matches sources. Good call.

## 5. Targeting / cost conventions

- **仁德 single-target-per-call**: reasonable, and does not change the power level. The generic
  `useSkill(cardIds[], targets[])` shape can't pair different cards with different recipients in one
  call (and `validateTargets` rejects duplicate targets), so "give A to X, B to Y" is two invocations
  — which is also how the paper game is played, one gift at a time. The heal still fires once at the
  cumulative ≥2 threshold via the turn flags, so nothing about the skill's effect changes. Fine.
- **`activeCardCount` (new field)**: additive and optional; `useSkill` checks
  `skill.activeCardCount !== undefined && cardIds.length !== activeCardCount`. I confirmed **no
  conflict with Batch B**: `kurou` (0 cards), `zhiheng` (any number), `qingnang` (its own 1-card cost)
  all leave `activeCardCount` undefined, so the new check is skipped for them and they behave exactly
  as before. 结姻 (2) and 离间 (1) use it. Backward-compatible. (Note: `qingnang` could adopt the field
  later, but that's a Batch B cleanup, not a 4.4 issue.) fanjian correctly does **not** set it — its
  card is chosen mid-effect via `chooseCard`, not passed up front.

## 6. Documented simplifications — all honestly described, correctly scoped

- **流离 no chained redirect**: honest. Re-firing `card.target` for the new target would require step 2
  to re-enter step 1, a bigger restructure than a content lane should do (§9). Accepted.
- **观星 top-only**: honest, and note this is a *real functional* gap, not cosmetic — the locale
  explicitly says "on top **or at the bottom**," and a player genuinely cannot bury a bad judgement
  card at the bottom in v1. But closing it needs a `Zone`/`putInZone` bottom-insert primitive, which
  is engine work §9 says a content lane must stop before. Correct to ship the useful top-reorder subset
  and flag it. Accepted.
- **离间 bypasses `card.play`**: honest (集智 won't fire for the synthesised duel; Standard has no
  interaction where that matters). Two small consequential side effects worth a note, both minor and
  edge: the synthesised duel is dispatched with `cards: []`, so (a) `duel.ts` logs an `undefined` card
  id on `log.plays_at` (an incomplete log line, same class as the `rainingArrows` gap CONTINUE.md
  already records — not a crash), and (b) the duel's damage carries `card: undefined`, so if the
  duellist happens to be 许褚, 裸衣's "+1 on 杀/决斗 damage" won't recognise it. Both are cosmetic/edge;
  fine to leave, worth a line in `lijian.ts`'s header.

## 7. Other observations (not blockers)

- **`orderTriggers` stays cold even with Batch C landing.** CONTINUE.md flags the client's missing
  `orderTriggers` prompt as a live stall risk "when Batch C lands." I checked: it does **not** become
  reachable. No Standard general has two triggers on one event, and a skill-vs-equipment collision on
  the same event (e.g. 铁骑 + 雌雄双股剑 both on `card.target`) is separated by the priority **bands**
  (skill 200 vs equipment 100–199), so `ambiguousOrderGroup` never fires. The client gap remains
  theoretical after this batch. Still worth the cheap insurance CONTINUE.md suggests eventually, but
  not a 4.4 blocker.
- **Five client-renderer gaps** (`guanxing`, `guicaiRetrial`, `yijiDistribute`, `liuliRedirect`,
  `declareSuit`): real, flagged in the impl handoff, and the same accepted class as 3.3/3.4/3.6 left
  for a 6.4b-style session. A table that reaches any of these five stalls on the asked player. Belongs
  to the next `PromptPanel`/`ChoicePanel` session; consistent with prior phases.
